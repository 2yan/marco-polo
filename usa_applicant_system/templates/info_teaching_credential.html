Getting Your Teaching Credential


Introduction

A teaching credential is a prerequisite for teaching English in China. We say this for a number of reasons. From a professional standpoint, it will help you become a better, more confident teacher. It will also make your application stand out to prospective employers, which will make you more employable. Finally, it is practically impossible to get a work visa without one.

You will hear three acronyms used frequently: TEFL, TESL, and TESOL. Here's what they mean:

  • TEFL = Teaching English as a Foreign Language. This is generally used to refer to teaching non-native speakers in an environment that doesn't speak English. (Example: Teaching English to Chinese students in China.)

  • TESL = Teaching English as a Second Language. This generally refers to teaching non-native speakers in an environment that does speak English. (Example: Teaching English to Chinese students in the USA.)

  • TESOL = Teaching English to Speakers of Other Languages. This is a more modern term that encompasses TESL and TEFL. It also acknowledges that English may be a student's third, fourth, or tenth language.

In order to qualify for a visa, you must get a 120-hour TEFL/TESOL certificate. If you do two units per day (which we recommend), you can finish the course in 11 days.

We assume that you have no formal background in education. If you have, say, a college degree in education, you may be overqualified for this program.
