Police Clearance

{% endblock %} {% block what %}

You need to get a police clearance, or a document from a law enforcement agency saying that you have no criminal record. We are going to have you get an FBI background check (see our explanation below). Your clearance must then be authenticated by the US Department of State and by the Chinese embassy.

{% endblock %} {% block why %}

In the United States, the term "law enforcement agency" can refer either to the state level or the federal level. As such, you have two choices for how to obtain your police clearance. A federal-level clearance (i.e. the FBI) is universally accepted, but it is more expensive and difficult to obtain. Going to your state's bureau of investigation is easier and cheaper, particularly if you live in your state's capital city. However, unlike a federal-level clearances, state-level clearances run the risk of being denied.

We say this because state-level clearances are arguably a loophole in the system. One could conceivably steal a car in Washington DC, drive across the Potomac into Virginia, and apply for a police clearance there. Since the car theft occurred in DC, the Virginia Bureau of Investigation would give the all-clear because the crime was not in their records. As time passes and foreigners commit more crimes in China, this loophole is likely to be closed. As such, we require all of our applicants to get a federal-level clearance.
