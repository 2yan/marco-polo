Medical Check

{% endblock %} {% block what %}

You must undergo a medical check before you go to China. You can do this at any health care provider (e.g. hospital, urgent care). The results are only valid for 6 months, so plan around your anticipated departure date.

The test will consist of:

  • EKG: Doesn't hurt. Measures your heart rate by placing diodes on your body.
  • Blood Test: Hurts. The doctor will draw three vials of blood from your arm.
  • Chest X-Ray: Doesn't hurt. Checks for tuberculosis and looks at your respiratory system.
{% endblock %} {% block why %}

It's normal for a government to ask you for a medical check if you're going to be staying there for an extended period of time. (Immigrants to the US have to do the same thing.)

Don't worry. Chances are you'll be fine. They are screening for contagious diseases that would endanger the general population, such as tuberculosis, HIV, and hepatitis.

  1. //////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////
  2. Download your Medical Packet. Fill out the appropriate fields.
  3. Go to a health care provider (////////////////////////), give them the form, and ask them to fill it out. Remind your doctor that it is requesting metric units.
  4. If your doctor seems confused by the form, tell them to write "Normal" in the appropriate boxes.
  5. They will do an EKG to measure your heart rate.
  6. They will do a blood test. If you're squeamish, tell the doctor beforehand so they can have you lay down.
  7. They will do a chest x-ray to evaluate your respiratory health.
  8. It will take about a week for the results to come back. When you receive the form, make sure they have placed a stamp in the "Official Stamp" space. The form is not valid otherwise.
  9. When you arrive in China, you will submit the original results of your medical exam. You may then required to do another medical test in a Chinese hospital, which is a lot more thorough.
